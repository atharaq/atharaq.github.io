Polar

  • Before last lesson, had you heard of polar coordinates?
  • If so, general opinion of the topic?

Idea

  • Describe points on the plane using \((r, \theta)\)
  • \(r\): “radius” (distance from origin)
  • \(\theta\): “angle” (when you draw a straight line to the point)
  • Useful if our motivating geometry is “circular” or “rotational”.

Playing with desmos

Graph of \(y = x^2 + a\) on Desmos

  • What happens when we change \(y = x^2\) to \(y = x^2 + a\)?
  • What about \(y = (x - b)^2 + a\)?
  • What about \(y = cx^2\)?
  • Similar questions: what if we graph \(r\) as a function of \(\theta\)?
  • Ex: \(r = \cos(3\theta)\).

Physics analogy

  • Q: what is a vector (in physics)?
  • A quantity which has magnitude and direction.
  • A 2 pound force horizontally pushing an object to the right is not the same as a 2 pound force pushing an object down.
  • Locations on the plane be thought of as vectors: the magnitude is \(r\), direction is \(\theta\).

Converting

  • Convert from \((x, y)\) coordinates to \((r, \theta)\) (and back)?
  • Example: express the point \((1, \sqrt{3})\) in polar coordinates.

Converting

  • \((x, y) \rightarrow (r, \theta)\):
    • \(r^2 = x^2 + y^2\)
    • \(\tan(\theta) = \frac{y}{x}\)
  • \((r, \theta) \rightarrow (x, y)\):
    • \(x = r \cos(\theta)\)
    • \(y = r \sin(\theta)\)

Calculus

  • Any polar curve \(r = f(\theta)\) can be converted to paramteric equations:
  • \(x = r\cos(\theta) = f(\theta)\cos(\theta)\)
  • \(y = r\sin(\theta) = f(\theta)\sin(\theta)\)
  • \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) can be found as before: \(\frac{y^\prime(\theta)}{x^\prime(\theta)}\)

Arc Lengths

  • Can use parametric: \(s = \int_a^b \sqrt{(x^\prime(\theta))^2 + (y^\prime(\theta))^2} d\theta\)
  • Plug in \(x = r\cos(\theta)\):
  • \(x^\prime(\theta) = r^\prime \cdot \cos(\theta) - r\sin(\theta)\)
  • \((x^\prime(\theta))^2 = (r^\prime)^2 \cos^2(\theta) - 2r^\prime \cdot r \cdot \cos(\theta)\sin(\theta) + r^2 \sin^2(\theta)\)
  • \(y = r\sin(\theta)\):
  • \(y^\prime = r^\prime \cdot \sin(\theta) + r\cos(\theta)\)
  • \((y^\prime)^2 = (r^\prime)^2 \sin^2(\theta) + 2r^\prime \cdot r \cos(\theta)\sin(\theta) + r^2 \cos^2(\theta)\)
  • Add them: \((r^\prime)^2(\cos^2(\theta) + \sin^2(\theta)) + r^2(\cos^2(\theta) + \sin^2(\theta))\)
  • Simplify: \((r^\prime)^2 + r^2\)!

Polar arc length

Arc length of a polar curve from \(\theta = a\) to \(\theta = b\): \(\int_a^b \sqrt{(r^\prime)^2 + r^2} d\theta\)

  • Example: \(r = \sin(2\theta)\) from \(\theta = 0\) to \(\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}\).
  • (One petal of the rose).
  • \(r^\prime = 2\cos(2\theta)\)
  • \((r^\prime)^2 = 4 \cos^2(2\theta)\)
  • \(r^2 = \sin^2(2\theta)\)
  • \(\int_0^{\pi/2} \sqrt{4\cos^2(2\theta) + \sin^2(2\theta)} d\theta \approx 2.42\)

Areas

Instead of approximating by rectangles, we approximate by sectors.

Circular Sectors

  • Area of a circle: \(A = \pi r^2\).
  • Area of a sector with angle \(\theta\): proportion! \(\frac{\theta}{2\pi} \cdot (\pi r^2) = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \theta\)

Formula

Approximate area as:

\[\sum_{i = 1}^n \frac{1}{2} r(\theta_i)^2 (\Delta \theta)\]

As \(n \rightarrow \infty\)? \(A = \int_a^b \frac{1}{2} r^2 d\theta\)

Example

Find the area of the region bounded by \(r = 2 \cos(2\theta)\) between \(\theta = -\frac{\pi}{4}\) and \(\theta = \frac{\pi}{4}\).

(graph)

Example

\[ \begin{align} A &= \int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \frac{1}{2} r^2 d\theta \\ &= \int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \frac{1}{2} (4\cos^2(2\theta)) d\theta \\ &= \int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} 2\cos^2(2\theta) d\theta \\ \end{align} \]

Example

  • Use \(\cos^2(2\theta) = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\cos(4\theta)\).
  • \(2\cos^2(2\theta) = 1 + \cos(4\theta)\).

\[ \begin{align} &\int_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} (1 + \cos(4\theta)) d\theta = \left.\frac{\sin(4\theta)}{4} + \theta \right|_{-\pi/4}^{\pi/4} \\ &= (\frac{\sin(\pi)}{4} + \frac{\pi}{4}) - (\frac{\sin(-\pi)}{4} - \frac{\pi}{4}) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{2} \end{align} \]
